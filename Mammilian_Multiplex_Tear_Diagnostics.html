The Challenge

Reimagining Disease Monitoring

Noncommunicable diseases — including cardiovascular, metabolic, and autoimmune disorders — account for nearly three-quarters of global deaths. Early detection is critical for improving outcomes, yet current diagnostic methods rely heavily on blood-based testing, requiring trained personnel, clinical infrastructure, and invasive sample collection.

These limitations drive increasing exploration of alternative biological fluids as diagnostic information sources. Tear fluid offers a compelling, stress-free alternative — reflecting systemic biomarker states without the discomfort or complexity of venipuncture.

Selected Analytes

Three Core Biomarkers

Biomarkers were selected for strong scientific grounding, presence across multiple inflammatory and metabolic states, and to support meaningful comparative analysis.

Interleukin-6 (IL-6)
Inflammatory Cytokine

Selected for its well-established role in inflammatory and immune regulation. IL-6 modulates immune cell stress response pathways and acute-phase immune system activity. Its presence across multiple inflammatory states makes it ideal for comparative diagnostics.

Glucose
Metabolic Marker

Chosen for its fundamental role in energy regulation and widespread use in monitoring metabolic health. Concentration is quantified via a competitive ELISA, producing a deep blue gradient where higher concentrations indicate lower glucose. Detected at clinically relevant concentrations.

Cortisol
Stress Hormone

A key biomarker for physiological stress, HPA axis signaling, and overall systemic homeostasis. Also considered for established laboratory measurement protocols (PPM) and significant scientific literature supporting its role in a wide range of systemic conditions.

Platform Architecture

Triplet Assay Efficacy

The modified Schirmer strip integrates three detection zones — enabling simultaneous blood, tear, and serum-level analyte correlation within a single collection pass.

Stress-Free Baseline Sampling

Allows biologists to use tear fluid as a reference material in a stress-free setting, eliminating physiological artifacts caused by handling stress common in traditional animal diagnostics.

Managing Chronic NCDs

Owners of pets could potentially use "At Home" or "Animal Strips" to monitor if their pet is developing chronic noncommunicable diseases over time.

Eliminating Confounding Variables

Blood glucose readings can fluctuate during stress-based sampling events, while tear measurements provide smooth biological signals during natural states.

Toxicity Screening

Researchers can detect organ toxicity much earlier than traditional invasive observations using tear biomarker elevation as an early warning signal.

Modeling Framework

Phenotypic Cube Modeling

A 3D coordinate system for physiological states — each axis represents a biomarker domain, and each node maps a unique combination of biomarker levels to a disease state.

1

Axis Development

Three axes were defined to correspond to inflammatory (IL-6), metabolic (glucose), and stress (cortisol) biomarker domains. These axes form the coordinate space of the diagnostic cube.

2

Disease Mapping

The 3D space was divided into a regular grid of 27 diagnostic nodes, where each node represents a unique combination of biomarker levels that maps to known or predicted disease states.

3

Symptoms Mapping

Scientific literature was used to identify a matrix of clinical references — biochemical signals used to position diseases with high confidence within the coordinate space.

4

UI–Software Integration

This model was converted into a functional diagnostic tool where users input biomarker values to receive immediate disease prediction, and color-coding represents biomarker confidence ranges.

5

Trajectory Mapping

Species differences in biomarker ranges were incorporated to adapt the cube for multiple mammalian species — using a private spherical shape as a predictor of future disease state.

Clinical & Research Utility

Platform Applications

TearScape's non-invasive architecture opens doors to monitoring scenarios previously impractical due to patient compliance, collection difficulty, or stress-induced sample contamination.

🐾

Veterinary Diagnostics

Non-invasive tear collection enables biomarker monitoring in animals where traditional blood draw causes significant physiological stress, rendering samples unreliable. Adaptable across bovine, feline, canine, and equine subjects with species-specific calibration.

🏠

At-Home NCD Monitoring

Consumer-ready strip format allows pet owners to conduct routine health checks at home, flagging early metabolic or inflammatory signals before clinical symptoms manifest — reducing emergency interventions and costs.

🔬

Research Baseline Studies

Stress-free sampling provides researchers with true resting-state biomarker readings, eliminating the confounding elevation of cortisol and glucose commonly introduced by restraint or needle-based collection protocols.

⚗️

Toxicity Screening

Early detection of organ toxicity — particularly hepatic and renal — is possible through tear biomarker elevation patterns, providing a real-time, minimally invasive safety signal during pharmaceutical and chemical exposure studies.

Detection Methodology

Colorimetric Analysis

Cortisol levels are measured via a competitive ELISA, producing a deep blue gradient inversely proportional to concentration. E-L nanoparticle aggregation assessed via a lysin assay provides real-time detection.

Blood and tear cortisol show an impressive linear relationship with R² = 0.98, establishing this as a highly dependable metric for metabolic tracking. Similarly, tear and serum cortisol levels show a robust positive correlation. The system removes subjectivity in colorimetric assessment by smoothing out the transient spikes often observed in plasma, making it remarkably suitable for detecting early-stage disease states.

Finalized Workflow

Five-Stage Diagnostic Process

1
Tear Collection
Modified Schirmer strip placed at lower eyelid for 60-second passive absorption
2
Reaction & Capture
Biomarker-specific capture antibodies bind analytes within the detection zones
3
Colorimetric Read
ELISA colorimetric signal analyzed — gradient intensity corresponds to concentration
4

5
Final Report
Disease risk score, trajectory mapping, and recommended clinical follow-up generated

Research Roadmap

Potential & Future Work

TearScape represents a proof-of-concept with broad translational potential. The platform's species-agnostic architecture enables rapid adaptation across veterinary and human clinical contexts.

Future iterations aim to increase diagnostic sensitivity, expand the biomarker panel, integrate AI-driven trajectory prediction, and miniaturize the detection hardware for field deployment — ultimately creating a portable, real-time, non-invasive diagnostic suite.

Expanded Biomarker Panel

Increase diagnostic sensitivity and cube resolution by adding lipocalin, lactoferrin, and additional cytokine targets.

AI Trajectory Prediction

Machine learning models trained on longitudinal tear data to predict disease progression curves ahead of symptom onset.

Human Clinical Trials

Translate mammalian validation data into human pilot studies, focusing on diabetic and autoimmune patient populations.

Portable Detection Hardware

Miniaturize the colorimetric reader into a smartphone-compatible device for field, home, and point-of-care deployment.

Multi-Species Calibration

Develop species-specific reference ranges for bovine, equine, feline, and canine populations to improve cube accuracy.

Longitudinal NCD Monitoring

Design subscription-based strip kits for at-home monitoring of chronic disease progression over months and years.
